Companies

Every business you automate. Switch between them, run their guided setup, or open their pages.

How this works
  • Each company lives in its own GitHub repository with its own content, schedule, captions and platform secrets.
  • Switch to makes a company the active workspace: the Dashboard, Scheduled Posts and Auto Posts sections then show that company.
  • Guided setup walks you through connecting Facebook, Instagram and GitHub step by step with verification at each step.

Your company list lives in this browser. Back it up to move it to another computer or keep it safe - tokens are included, so store the file privately.

Scheduled posts (queue)

Everything waiting to publish for the active company, with its date, time and status.

How this works

  • Pending items can be cancelled; failed items show their error and retry up to three times.

Auto posts (campaigns)

Hands-free posting, one month at a time: tick a month's checkbox, give it a start date, choose its platforms, and it posts itself day by day - feed posts and stories.

How this works
  • A campaign is a folder under content/. Months (Month 1, Month 2...) hold day folders; each day holds a Post and a Story folder of images/videos.
  • Each month has its own checkbox, start date and platform selection: only checked months post, day 1 on the month's start date, day 2 the next day, and so on.
  • Within one day: 2 files go out 6 hours apart, 3 files 4 hours apart, 4+ files 3 hours apart, starting at the posts/stories times.
  • Captions are written automatically with brand, niche and viral hashtags plus your contact details (or use a photo1.txt / caption.txt of your own). Stories have no caption.
  • Feed photos and stories both publish to Instagram and Facebook; every attempt is recorded in the Dashboard activity log.
  • Safety: long-past dates are not flooded out, and at most 20 posts go out per 24 hours.

Bulk upload and auto-sort

Drop a pile of unsorted photos here and they are sorted into a month of day folders, ready to schedule.

How this works
  • Pick the campaign and the month to fill: an existing month continues its own day numbering; a new month (e.g. Month 2) starts at Day 1.
  • Feed images fill each day's Post folder (2 or 3 per day) and story images the Story folder; everything is sized and committed in one go.
  • New campaigns and months are registered automatically, unchecked, so nothing posts until you set the month's date, tick it, and Save.

Reports

How the posted content is performing - likes, comments and reach - pulled straight from Instagram and Facebook. Share the clean client-facing version with the business.

How this works
  • An automated weekly job (and the button below) reads every published post's live numbers into data/report.json.
  • The client report is a separate, branding-only page with no controls - safe to send to the business as a link.
  • Story numbers differ from feed numbers; whatever a platform will not give is simply left blank.

Add a new company

Creates a complete, ready-to-run copy of this automation for a new client: repository (on the main branch), workflows, dashboard and personalized captions.

How this works, step by step
  • A new GitHub repository is created automatically from this one as a template, named after the company (lowercase with hyphens), public, with main as the default branch and all starter files committed to it.
  • The business profile (name, description, phone, website) is written into config.yaml - captions use it automatically.
  • Starter content and queue data are cleared so the client begins empty.
  • The company then appears under Companies with a Guided setup button that walks you through connecting Facebook, Instagram and GitHub Pages.
  • The token needs repository-creation rights: a fine-grained PAT with Administration + Contents read/write, or a classic token with the repo scope.

User manual

How to run the system day to day, in plain language. For connecting accounts and credentials, see Setup Instructions instead.

What this system does

It posts your marketing photos to Instagram and Facebook automatically - feed posts and stories - one month of content at a time. You load a month of photos once, pick a start date, tick the month, and it posts every day by itself: captions are written automatically with your phone number, website and hashtags, and every post is mirrored to Facebook (stories land in the Page's Stories ring). A robot on GitHub wakes up every 15 minutes and does the work; this dashboard is your remote control.

Getting around the dashboard
  • Dashboard - the day's numbers, the activity log (every post and any errors), and the latest automation runs.
  • Scheduled Posts - everything waiting to publish, with dates and cancel buttons.
  • Auto Posts - your campaigns and months; this is where you load content and switch months on.
  • Companies - switch between the businesses you manage and run their guided setup.
  • Admin - add a brand-new company (its repository is created automatically).
  • User Manual / Setup Instructions - this guide, and the credentials guide.
  • Settings - the GitHub connection for the active company.

The left menu's Active company chip always shows which business you are working on.

Post a month of content (the everyday job)
  1. Open Auto Posts.
  2. Scroll to Bulk upload and auto-sort. Pick the campaign (or type a new name), and keep the suggested month (e.g. Month 2).
  3. Choose your feed images (the polished ones for the grid) and your story images (the casual ones). Pick 2 or 3 per day for each.
  4. Press Preview plan to see how they will be split into days, then Sort and upload.
  5. When the upload finishes, find the new month card above. Set its start date, tick Instagram and Facebook, tick the month's checkbox, and press Save campaign.
  6. Done. Day 1 posts on the start date, day 2 the next day, and so on. Open the Day-by-day plan inside the month card any time to see exactly what posts when.

Timing: feed posts start at the campaign's "Posts time", stories at the "Stories time". If a day has 2 files they go out 6 hours apart; 3 files, 4 hours apart.

Pause everything (and resume)

On the Dashboard there is a big Pause automation button. Press it and the whole company stops instantly: no posts go out and no new content is queued. A red banner appears across the top so it is obvious. Nothing is lost - your months, dates and queued posts stay exactly as they were.

Press Resume automation to switch it back on; due posts go out on the next 15-minute run. Pausing is per company (it affects the one shown in the "Active company" chip), so pausing one client never affects another.

Fix a mistake without pausing everything
  • Pause just one month: untick its checkbox in Auto Posts and Save. Already-queued posts can be cancelled under Scheduled Posts.
  • Cancel one post: Scheduled Posts, press Cancel on the row. The photo is removed too.
  • Wrong start date before anything posted: change the date and Save - future days follow the new date. Posts already in the queue keep their old time; cancel them if needed and they re-queue on the next run.
  • Post something right now: Scheduled Posts, Process due posts now pushes out anything overdue.
Do tokens ever expire? (No.)

Both Instagram and Facebook use tokens that renew themselves. A weekly job refreshes the Instagram token and re-derives a permanent Facebook Page token, writing the fresh values back automatically. Once a company is set up, you never log in again or re-paste a token - it just keeps running until you pause it.

Reading the activity log

Dashboard, Activity log. Every attempt is one row: when, which platform, feed or story, and the result. A green posted row shows the platform's post id. A red failed row shows the platform's own error message - read it, it usually says exactly what is wrong (for example an expired token or a permission the Page is missing). Failed posts retry automatically up to three times.

Add a new company (about 30 minutes end to end)
  1. Admin: fill in the business name, what it does, phone and website, then Add Company. Its repository is created automatically on the main branch, captions personalized, starter data cleared.
  2. Companies: press Guided setup on the new card. The wizard walks you through the GitHub token, Facebook Page, Instagram and GitHub Pages, verifying each step - all tokens are made long-lived or permanent automatically.
  3. Auto Posts: bulk-upload their first month, set the date and platforms, tick, Save.

From then on the new company runs itself, exactly like the first one.

Good-to-know rules the system enforces
  • Photos are converted and resized automatically; feed keeps its natural shape, stories become 1080x1920.
  • Each photo is deleted from storage after it posts - the repo does not fill up.
  • Captions never repeat back to back, always include your contact line, and stay within Instagram's limits.
  • A month whose dates are long past will not flood out old posts; fix the start date and it schedules from there.
  • Instagram tokens renew themselves weekly; permanent Facebook Page tokens never expire.

Setup instructions

Everything needed to connect a company, in plain language. These are the exact steps used to configure the first working company. For a guided version with verification, use the Guided setup button on any company card.

1. Create a Facebook App and Page

The Page is where posts appear. The App is what gives the automation permission to post.

  1. Create the Page (once, from your personal Facebook account): go to https://www.facebook.com/pages/create, enter the business name, pick a category (e.g. Furniture shop), press Create Page, then Publish.
  2. Create the App: go to https://developers.facebook.com, click My Apps, then Create App, choose type Business, name it (e.g. "Company Poster") and press Create App.
  3. Tip: choose "I don't want to connect a business portfolio" if asked - personally-owned apps avoid Business Manager permission problems.
  4. On the app's App settings, Basic page, note the App ID and App Secret (click Show).
2. Get the Facebook Page ID and Page Access Token
  1. Open https://developers.facebook.com/tools/explorer (Graph API Explorer) and select your app top-right.
  2. Click Generate Access Token. Approve the permissions pages_show_list, pages_manage_posts, pages_read_engagement.
  3. Important: on the "Which Pages do you want to use" screen, tick your Page. Do not choose "opt in later".
  4. In the request box type me/accounts and press Submit. The response lists your Page with its id (that is FACEBOOK_PAGE_ID) and an access_token.
  5. If me/accounts returns an empty list, use the direct route that is proven to work: find the Page id in the token debugger (https://developers.facebook.com/tools/debug/accesstoken - paste your token, look under Granular Scopes), then query PAGE_ID?fields=access_token,name in the Explorer.
  6. Make the token permanent: exchange your user token at https://graph.facebook.com/v21.0/oauth/access_token?grant_type=fb_exchange_token&client_id=APP_ID&client_secret=APP_SECRET&fb_exchange_token=YOUR_TOKEN, then repeat step 4 or 5 with the returned long-lived token - the Page token it yields does not expire.

The Guided setup modal does the exchange and verification for you automatically.

3. Link the Instagram Business account to the Facebook Page
  1. In the Instagram app: Profile, menu, Settings and privacy, Account type and tools, Switch to professional account, choose Business.
  2. On your Facebook Page: Settings, Linked accounts, Instagram, Connect account, and log in with the Instagram business account.
  3. This link is what lets Meta issue posting tokens for the Instagram account.
4. Get the Instagram Access Token, User ID and App Secret
  1. In your Meta app (https://developers.facebook.com, My Apps, your app): click Add Product and add Instagram, then the Instagram Business Login (API setup with Instagram login) use case.
  2. Under the Instagram product's API setup, click Generate token next to your Instagram account and approve instagram_business_basic and instagram_business_content_publish. The token starts with IGAA.
  3. Get the User ID: open https://graph.instagram.com/me?fields=id,username&access_token=YOUR_TOKEN in a browser tab; the id field is INSTAGRAM_USER_ID.
  4. The App Secret is on App settings, Basic (click Show) - store it as INSTAGRAM_APP_SECRET; it lets the system upgrade and refresh the token automatically so it never expires.
5. Generate a GitHub fine-grained token (GH_PAT)
  1. Go to https://github.com/settings/personal-access-tokens/new.
  2. Token name: e.g. "company-posts-automation". Expiration: choose No expiration (or 1 year and note the renewal date).
  3. Repository access: Only select repositories, pick the company's repo.
  4. Repository permissions: set Actions, Contents, Secrets, Workflows and Administration all to Read and write. (Workflows is needed to copy the automation workflow files into a new repo; Administration lets Guided setup set the default branch.)
  5. Click Generate token and copy it (it is shown once).
  6. This token is used twice: pasted into this dashboard's Settings (so the dashboard can act on the repo), and stored as the GH_PAT secret (so workflows can refresh platform tokens).
6. Enable GitHub Pages (main branch, /docs folder)
  1. Open the company repository on GitHub and click Settings.
  2. In the left menu click Pages.
  3. Under Build and deployment, Source, choose Deploy from a branch.
  4. Set Branch to main and the folder to /docs, then press Save.
  5. Wait about a minute; the published URL appears at the top of the Pages screen (like https://OWNER.github.io/REPO/). That URL is this dashboard, hosted.

No main branch or /docs folder to choose? The repository is empty. Companies created from Admin, Add Company already have both. For a repository you made by hand, open Companies, Guided setup and use the Create the main branch and /docs folder button on the GitHub Pages step - it copies the code, workflows and dashboard onto main so these options appear.

7. Where every secret is stored

All platform credentials live as encrypted repository secrets: repo Settings, Secrets and variables, Actions, New repository secret. Exact names:

  • INSTAGRAM_ACCESS_TOKEN - Instagram token starting with IGAA
  • INSTAGRAM_USER_ID - Instagram Business account ID
  • INSTAGRAM_APP_SECRET - Meta app secret (enables automatic token refresh)
  • GH_PAT - the fine-grained token from section 5
  • FACEBOOK_PAGE_ID - the Page's numeric ID
  • FACEBOOK_PAGE_ACCESS_TOKEN - the permanent Page token
  • FACEBOOK_APP_ID, FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET, FACEBOOK_USER_TOKEN - let the weekly job re-derive the Page token so it never expires
  • ANTHROPIC_API_KEY - optional, for AI-written standard posts
8. How token auto-refresh works (and why the App Secret is needed)

Instagram long-lived tokens last 60 days. A scheduled workflow (Refresh social tokens, Sundays) calls Instagram's refresh endpoint and writes the renewed token back into the INSTAGRAM_ACCESS_TOKEN secret using GH_PAT - so the 60-day clock resets every week, forever.

The INSTAGRAM_APP_SECRET is required for one thing: upgrading a fresh short-lived token (1 hour) to a long-lived one (60 days). With it stored, you can paste any newly generated token and the system upgrades it automatically at post time and on the next refresh run.

Facebook works the same way. A Page token derived from a long-lived User token never expires - but if a short-lived one is ever pasted by mistake, the same weekly job re-derives a fresh permanent Page token from FACEBOOK_USER_TOKEN using FACEBOOK_APP_ID and FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET, and re-stores FACEBOOK_PAGE_ACCESS_TOKEN. Store those three and Facebook can never lapse again. (The one-time symptom where a token "expired" was a short-lived token stored without these three.)

Connection settings

How this works
  • The dashboard is a plain web page: it talks directly to the GitHub API with the token below. There is no other server, and the token is stored only in this browser.
